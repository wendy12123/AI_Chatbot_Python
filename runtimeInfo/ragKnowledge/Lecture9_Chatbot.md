# AI Knowledge Base: Chatbots and Conversational AI

## 1. Definition and History

Chatbots are software applications designed to simulate human conversation, primarily for user interaction over the internet.

* **Turing Test (1950):** Alan Turing published "Computing Machinery and Intelligence," introducing the concept of machines simulating human language.
* **ELIZA (1966):** The first chatbot, which used keyword matching to simulate a psychotherapist.
* **PARRY (1972):** A chatbot designed to simulate a person with paranoid schizophrenia.
* **Modern Era (2010+):** The rise of voice-activated assistants (Siri, Alexa, Google Assistant) and sophisticated Al-powered conversational bots using NLP and Large Language Models (LLMs).

---

## 2. Types of Chatbots

Chatbots are generally categorized by how they process information and interact with users:

* **Rule-Based Chatbots:** Follow a predefined set of rules or decision trees. They are limited to specific paths and cannot handle complex or unexpected queries.
* **AI-Powered (Conversational) Chatbots:** Utilize Natural Language Processing (NLP) and Machine Learning to understand intent, context, and sentiment. They can learn from interactions to improve over time.

---

## 3. Core Terminology & Parameters

When developing or interacting with modern AI-based chatbots, several technical parameters define their behavior:

### Role Definition

In a chat-based model, each message is assigned a "role" to help the AI understand the structure of the conversation:

* **System:** Provides high-level instructions to the AI (e.g., "You are a helpful assistant").
* **User:** The messages sent by the human.
* **Assistant:** The responses generated by the AI model.

### The Temperature Parameter

Temperature is a configuration setting (typically a float from 0 to 2) that controls the "randomness" or "creativity" of the output:

* **0.0 – 0.3:** Deterministic and factual. The model will likely give the same answer to the same prompt every time.
* **0.7:** Balanced. Provides a mix of consistency and natural variation.
* **1.0+:** Creative and random. Useful for brainstorming or storytelling, but increases the risk of hallucinations.

## 4. Development Challenges

Building effective chatbots involves overcoming several technical and linguistic hurdles:

* **Context Management:** Maintaining the history of a conversation so the bot remembers what was said in previous turns.
* **Handling Ambiguity:** Determining user intent when a request is vague or has multiple meanings.
* **Integration:** Connecting the chatbot to external APIs or databases (RAG) to provide real-time, accurate information.

---

## 5. Building Tools

Modern platforms allow for "low-code" or "no-code" chatbot development:

* **Botpress:** A popular tool for building and deploying conversational AI. It allows developers to start from scratch or use templates to create complex conversation flows.
* **Google Colab:** Frequently used for testing Python-based chatbot logic using libraries like `openai` or `chatterbot`.
